I have always had a different color description; Black is multidimensional and cloudy like my darkest thoughts. Blue is transparent like a glass sky. Green is wet like grass underneath the water. My story began when someone told me I sketched human hair 'in a very graphic way' and made me realize a graphic designer was hidden in me. When I grew up, studying graphic design wasn't an option, so I ended up getting a master's degree in TESOL— not my best decision, but it ended up working in my favor later on in my journey.

I've heard people saying, "it takes wars to go after one's passion," and never thought it would be pretty literal in my case. When the war started in Syria, I had to leave my home country and immigrate to Canada. Landing in Ottawa, I looked at the bright side of it and thought to myself: this is my clue, I can study graphic design— thanks to the master's degree that spared me the language barrier!

Here at Algonquin College, I was taught by top professors who helped refine my skills and develop my talent. I have learned so much and will continue learning, but I am excited to show you what I've been up to so far.

About me
