My story began when I sketched human hair in a graphic way, and realized a little graphic designer was hidden in me. When I grew up, graphic design wasn't an option, so I ended up getting a master's degree in TESOL— very weird, I know! This ended up working in my favor through my journey.

I've heared people saying "it takes wars to go after one's passion" and never thought it would be pretty literal in my case. When the war started in Syria, I had to leave my home country and immigrate to Canada. Landed in Ottawa, I looked at the bright side of it and thought to myself: this is my clue, I can study graphic design, and I won't have to learn the language— thanks to the master's degree that I thought I'll never appreciate!

Here at Algonquin College, I was taught by top professors who helped refine my skills and develop my talent. I'm excited to show you what I've learned so far.
