My story began when someone told me I sketched human hair 'in a very graphic way' and made me realize a graphic designer was hidden in me. When I grew up, studying graphic design wasn't an option, so I ended up getting a master's degree in TESOL— very weird, but it ended up working in my favor late in my journey.

I've heard people saying, "it takes wars to go after one's passion," and never thought it would be pretty literal in my case. When the war started in Syria, I had to leave my home country and immigrate to Canada. Landing in Ottawa, I looked at the bright side of it and thought to myself: this is my clue, I can study graphic design— thanks to the master's degree that spared me the language barrier!

Here at Algonquin College, I was taught by top professors who helped refine my skills and develop my talent. I'm excited to show you what I've learned so far.

About me

Hello, I'm Inas— I probably should write it the way I pronounce it— 'Ines.' I am a Graphic Design Student at Algonquin College in Ottawa, Canada. I am skilled in the Adobe Creative Suite, including After Effects, Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign. I have a humble knowledge of coding in HTML and CSS— I'm grateful for this knowledge as I could code this website! This portfolio includes glimpses of my work so far.

I try hard, I actively chase learning opportunities, and I may not know it all— just yet, but nothing can stop me from asking Google!
